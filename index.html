Ketek Writer

Write the first half. Watch the mirror follow you, word for word, in grey. Edit any mirrored word to fix its tense, plurality, or punctuation — it stays locked to that word as you keep writing. Hover a word to see its mirror partner light up.

The traditional ketek form: five sections, each meant to read as its own complete thought. This is a simplified, single-pivot take on that structure — not a strict reproduction of Vorin poetics.
